Green Bursa Tour - Arabic

A trip to Bursa from Istanbul is suitable for all ages, whether adults or children, everyone can enjoy it. Green Bursa is the fourth largest city in Turkey by population and the largest industrial city in Turkey, playing a major role in the Turkish economy. It is located in northwestern Turkey, south of the Sea of Marmara, between Istanbul, Ankara, and Izmir, near the city of Yalova. It was the capital of the Ottoman Empire between 1326 and 1365 AD.

Mount Uludag is the largest mountain in Bursa, known as the Great Mountain. Its highest peak reaches 2530 meters above sea level and is called the Black Summit. In winter, the snow can reach 3 meters high, where international skiing competitions are held. You can reach Mount Uludag via tourist trips or cable car, through which you can view the natural scenery and reach your destination faster.

The mountain features cable cars divided into two types: a long cable car extending over 9 kilometers at a height of 250 meters, and a short cable car extending over half the distance, 4.5 kilometers.

The Silk Market "Koza Han" is one of the most famous Turkish tourist attractions in Bursa. The market is located in a well-known place between Orhan Bey Mosque and the Grand Mosque. It was opened in 1491 by Sultan Bayezid II. Koza Han became a center for silk trade, hence carrying the name of this trade and being called the Silk Market.

Bursa has been famous since ancient times as one of the most important manufacturers of Turkish delight. We provide you the opportunity to learn how this delicious popular candy is made, enter its factory, and see it in its early stages of production. There are different types of Turkish delight, some made from honey, pomegranate, or cocoa and many others, some with various nuts or plain.
